History of Subaru

Subaruwas founded in the early 20th century in Japan, under the parent company Fuji Heavy Industries. The first Subaru car model, the Subaru 1500, debuted in 1954. Subaru's name is inspired by the Pleiades star cluster, which is featured in its logo. The brand gained international acclaim for its use of boxer engines and the development of symmetrical all-wheel drive systems. These technological advancements set Subaru apart, offering enhanced stability, performance, and safety. The Subaru Leone launched in the 1970s, which developed a reputation for use in rugged terrains and harsh weather conditions and made the brand successful in the United States. Through its history, Subaru has grown from a small manufacturer to a global automotive giant, with a loyal customer base and a strong commitment to innovation, safety, and eco-consciousness.Models like the Impreza, Forester, and Outback have become iconic, embodying the brand's commitment to customer satisfaction.
